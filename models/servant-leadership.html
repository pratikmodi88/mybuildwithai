The 'Why'

Traditional leadership focuses on the accumulation and exercise of power by one at the “top of the pyramid.” Servant leadership flips this, focusing primarily on the growth and well-being of people and the communities to which they belong.

Key Components

  • Empathy: Seeking to understand and empathize with others.
  • Healing: Supporting the emotional and professional well-being of the team.
  • Stewardship: Holding the organization's assets and people in trust for the greater good.
  • Growth: A deep commitment to the personal and professional growth of every individual.

Practical Application

In your next team meeting, instead of giving orders, ask: "What can I do this week to make your job easier or remove a barrier for you?" Shift from being the person who is served to the person who serves the mission.

Chosen Insight: Simon Sinek explains why the best leaders are those who put their people first, mirroring the core of Greenleaf's original philosophy.
