The 'Why'

One leadership style does not fit all. This model solves the problem of rigid management by providing a framework to adapt your behavior based on the specific maturity and skill level of your team members.

Key Components

  • Directing (S1): High direction, low support for enthusiastic beginners.
  • Coaching (S2): High direction, high support for disillusioned learners.
  • Supporting (S3): Low direction, high support for capable but cautious performers.
  • Delegating (S4): Low direction, low support for self-reliant achievers.

Practical Application

Before your next 1-on-1, map your team member to a quadrant. If they have mastered a task, intentionally shift from S2 to S3 to build their confidence through autonomy.
