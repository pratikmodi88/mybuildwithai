The $1 Trillion Knowledge Engine

How Morgan Stanley scaled their expert knowledge base to 100,000+ documents, giving financial advisors a "superpower" in seconds.

01

The Pain: Data Overload

Morgan Stanley manages a massive library of 100,000+ proprietary research reports and investment strategies. For advisors, finding a specific market insight during a client call was like finding a needle in a haystack.

02

The Spark: AI @ Morgan Stanley

They built an internal GPT-4 powered assistant that uses Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). Instead of advisors searching folders, they simply ask the AI questions based on the firm's private research.

03

The Win: Advisor Efficiency

The system evolved from processing 7,000 questions to answering queries against their entire 100,000-document corpus. Advisors can now access expert insights instantly, keeping them focused on client relationships.

The How: Knowledge Orchestration

Enterprise RAG Architecture

Repository 100,000+ Proprietary Reports
Retrieval Layer GPT-4 Context Processing
Final Output Expert Investment Advice

By layering GPT-4 over their private data, Morgan Stanley ensures responses are grounded in verified internal research, preventing the hallucinations common in public AI models.
