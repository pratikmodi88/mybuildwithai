Situational Leadership

Developed by Paul Hersey and Ken Blanchard, this model suggests that there is no "single best" style of leadership. Instead, the most effective leaders adapt their style to the Performance Readiness of the person they are leading.

The Four Styles

  • S1: Telling (High Task, Low Relationship) – For people who lack competence but are committed.
  • S2: Selling (High Task, High Relationship) – For people who have some competence but lack commitment.
  • S3: Participating (Low Task, High Relationship) – For people who have competence but lack confidence.
  • S4: Delegating (Low Task, Low Relationship) – For people who are both competent and committed.

How to use this today:

Pick one team member. Ask yourself: "Do they have the skills for this specific task, and do they have the confidence?" If they are new and nervous, use S1. If they are a pro, use S4 and step back.
